Social Media is a collective of online communication channels dedicated to the contribution of community, collaboration, content sharing and interaction. Web sites and applications, devoted to the forums, microblogging, social networking, social bookmarking, social conservation and wikis are among the different types of social media.

Popular social media sites includes: Facebook, Twitter, Google+, Reddit, Pinterest, YouTube, LinkedIn, etc.

Social Media Policies for Your Business

The importance of using social media to help any business grow, can not be understated. But there may be serious legal consequences for companies when their employees or contractors and suppliers use any of the popular social media forums. This may be true as when employees act on behalf of their business, and when they use social media for their personal use.

Smart business owners identify problems ahead of time, and then develop a strategy to prevent unnecessary liability and address risks when they become known. Of course, this strategy should begin with policies for social media. However, many companies produce social media policies, which does not contain any potential problems that need, or even policy projects thus making them illegal.

If you have employees or use a different type of third-party vendors or affiliates, you should adopt a written policy of social media. Although not an absolute shield from liability, companies must adopt policies in the field of social work of the media to protect the employer in accordance with the organizational culture of the company.
